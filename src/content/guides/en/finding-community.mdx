## Hashtag Strategy (2 minutes)

### Why Hashtags Matter

Hashtags are the primary way to discover content and people on Nostr:

**Benefits:**

- Find people with shared interests
- Join ongoing conversations
- Get discovered by others
- Build topical authority

### Popular Nostr Hashtags

**General:**

- #introductions - Say hello
- #asknostr - Ask questions
- #nostr - General Nostr discussion

**Technology:**

- #bitcoin - Bitcoin discussion
- #lightning - Lightning Network
- #privacy - Privacy tools
- #opensource - Open source software

**Lifestyle:**

- #photography - Photos
- #music - Music sharing
- #art - Visual art
- #writing - Long-form content
- #foodstr - Food and cooking
- #coffeechain - Coffee culture
- #memes - Memes and humor

**Community & Culture:**

- #grownostr - Gardening and growing
- #semester - Education and learning
- #plebchain - Bitcoin plebs community

**Regional:**

- #africa, #asia, #europe, #americas
- City-specific tags
- Language tags

### Using Hashtags Effectively

**Do:**

- Use 2-5 relevant hashtags per post
- Check trending tags in your client
- Follow hashtags you're interested in
- Create your own for events/series

**Don't:**

- Spam with 10+ hashtags
- Use irrelevant tags
- Tag every single post
- Create duplicate/similar tags

### Discovering New Hashtags

1. **Check what others use** - See what hashtags popular accounts in your niche use
2. **Search and explore** - Try searching different topics
3. **Ask the community** - "What hashtags should I follow for [topic]?"

<Callout type="tip">
  **Pro tip:** Mix broad tags (#bitcoin) with specific ones (#bitcoinmining) to
  reach different audiences.
</Callout>

## Following the Right People (3 minutes)

### Quality Over Quantity

**Better to follow 50 great accounts than 5,000 random ones.**

### What to Look For

**Good follows:**

- Post consistently (not too much, not too little)
- Share valuable content
- Engage with community
- Have been around a while
- Match your interests

**Red flags:**

- Only self-promotion
- Spam or low-quality posts
- Never engage with others
- Brand new accounts (no history)
- Suspicious behavior

## DM Etiquette (2 minutes)

### Safety First

**Nostr DMs are encrypted but not perfect:**

- End-to-end encrypted (E2EE)
- But metadata visible (who talks to whom)
- Some clients handle encryption differently

### DM Best Practices

**Do:**

- Be respectful
- Introduce yourself
- Keep it relevant
- Respect boundaries
- Remember it's still public metadata

**Don't:**

- Spam or harass
- Send unsolicited messages
- Share private keys (EVER)
- Assume complete privacy
- Send sensitive info

### Handling Unwanted DMs

**If someone bothers you:**

1. **Mute** - Stop seeing their content
2. **Block** - They can't interact with you
3. **Report** - If serious, tell community
4. **Don't engage** - Feeds trolls

**Prevention:**

- Don't post your DMs are open if you don't mean it
- Be selective about who you respond to
- Trust your instincts

## Building Your Network (3 minutes)

### Engagement Strategy

**The 80/20 Rule:**

- 80% adding value (posts, replies, zaps)
- 20% self-promotion

**Ways to Add Value:**

- Thoughtful replies
- Sharing insights
- Answering questions
- Curating content
- Encouraging others

### Consistency Matters

**Post regularly:**

- Daily is great
- Few times a week is good
- Weekly minimum
- Quality over quantity

**Best times to post:**

- Varies by your audience
- Experiment and see
- Generally: morning/evening
- Avoid middle of night (unless that's your timezone)

### Building Relationships

**One-on-one:**

- Reply to their posts
- Zap their content
- Send thoughtful DMs
- Remember details
- Be genuine

**Community:**

- Participate in conversations
- Help newcomers
- Share opportunities
- Celebrate others' wins

### Patience

**It takes time:**

- First 100 followers are hardest
- Momentum builds slowly
- Quality relationships > quantity
- Be in it for the long term

<Callout type="info">
  **Community > Audience.** Build genuine connections, not just follower counts.
</Callout>
